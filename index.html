Biography

Missile Man


  • Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He played a crucial role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs.
  • Dr. Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He obtained a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • During his career, Dr. Kalam made significant contributions to India's space and defense programs. He was often referred to as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. He was also involved in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
  • Aside from his scientific achievements, Dr. Abdul Kalam was a renowned motivational speaker and writer. He authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," which inspired millions of people, especially the youth, to pursue their dreams and contribute to the development of India.
  • Dr. Kalam received numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and several honorary doctorates from universities around the world.

Legacy

On 25th July 2002, when Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was sworn in as the 11th President of the Republic of India, he became the first scientist and the first bachelor to occupy the office of Rashtrapati Bhawan. In the next five years, the missile man of India demonstrated his abilities as a true People’s president or even ‘Children’s President’. Known for his pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, Dr. Kalam has been an inspirational figure for everyone, whether a child, youth or adult.
